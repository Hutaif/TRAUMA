Treatment
  • Treatment by type:
    Acute anterior: closed reduction should be attempted
    • Patient is placed supine with a roll between the scapulae to draw shoulders back
    • Direct pressure is applied to the medial clavicle
    • May be left unreduced if closed reduction cannot be achieved
    • Recurrent subluxation is common but usually asymptomatic
    • Figure-of-eight straps can be used post-reduction for 4-6 weeks to allow for ligament healing

    Acute posterior: closed reduction under anesthesia
    • Thoracic surgery back-up should be obtained as medial clavicle may be acting to tamponade a compromised vessel
    • Patient is placed supine with a roll between the scapulae
      • Abduct and extend arm
      • A towel clip may be required to apply anterior traction on the clavicle
    • Intraoperative ultrasound can confirm reduction
    • Open reduction and reconstruction of SC ligaments is indicated if closed/percutaneous attempts fail
    • Figure of eight semitendinosus autograft technique to reconstruct the capsule can be used
      • Pin fixation has been associated with complications related to hardware migration and are discouraged

    Chronic anterior: nonoperative

    Chronic posterior (> 7 days): avoid closed reduction due to possible adhesions with underlying structures
    • Open reduction with thoracic surgery back up

Complications
  • Cosmetic: enlarged medial prominence due to unreducible anterior dislocation or recurrent subluxation
  • Posterior dislocations have complication rates of up to 20% including:
    • Carotid artery compression
    • Esophageal rupture
    • Laceration of superior vena cava
    • Pneumothorax
    • Subclavian artery compression
    • Thoracic outlet syndrome
    • Venous compression
