Imaging
    Radiographs
  • AP, scapular Y (45° from coronal plane) and axillary (shoulder abducted 70° and beam shot through the axilla) views of the shoulder are standard evaluations
  • AP: absence of normal glenohumeral overlap suggests dislocation
    • Proximal humerus is typically internally rotated in posterior dislocations, losing the profile of the neck
  • Axillary: required to confirm reduction
    • Normal AP shoulder
    Normal scapular Y view
    Normal axillary shoulder
  • Velpeau view: alternative if axillary not possible
    • Patient standing, leaning backward over plate on table, beam perpendicular to plate
  • West Point view: visualize the anteroinferior glenoid rim
    • Patient is prone, beam 25° from horizontal, 25° medial
  • Hill-Sachs view: AP with arm in maximal ER
  • Stryker notch view: visualize posterior lateral defects
    • Patient is supine, hand on head, elbow straight up, beam 10° cephalad directed at corocoid

  • CT: evaluate proximal humerus and glenoid lesions
  • MRI: evaluate rotator cuff, Bankart, ligamentous or capsular lesions
