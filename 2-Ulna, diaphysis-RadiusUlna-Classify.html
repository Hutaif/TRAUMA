  • Bado classification: Monteggia fractures
    Type I: anterior dislocation of radial head with anterior angulation of ulna fracture
    Type II: posterior/posterolateral dislocation of radial head with posterior angulation (most common in adults)
    IIA: fracture at the level of the coronoid
    IIB: fracture just distal to coronoid
    IIC: diaphyseal fracture
    IID: complex fractures that may involve the proximal third of the ulna
    Type III: lateral/anterolateral dislocation of radial head
    Type IV: anterior dislocation of the radial head with fractures of both the radius and ulna at the same level
