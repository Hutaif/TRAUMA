Classification
  • Important for selecting the appropriate surgical approach as no single approach provides access to all parts of the acetabulum

  • Judet-Letournel: five elementary and five associated patterns based on columnar damage
    Elementary patterns:
    • Posterior wall: can involve marginal impaction of cartilage into cancellous bone
      • Gull sign may be present
    • Posterior column: ilioischial line disrupted
      • Posterior column often detaches in one piece
    • Anterior wall: teardrop often displaced medially
    • Anterior column: iliopectineal line disrupted
      • Defined by level at which innominate bone is divided (low, medium, high) with higher involvement incorporating the weight-bearing portion
    • Transverse: ilioischial line and teardrop maintain normal relationship but ilioischial and iliopectineal lines are disrupted
      • Innominate bone divided into two fragments in one of the following ways:
        - Transtectal: through the acetebular dome
        - Juxtatectal: through the junction of the acetabular dome and the fossa acetabuli (center of the acetabulum, contains ligamentum teres)
        - Infratectal: through the fossa acetabuli
      • Elementary patterns
    Associated patterns:
    • Posterior column and posterior wall: associated with posterior hip dislocation complicated by sciatic nerve injury
      • Posterior wall fragment is commonly displaced and rotated
    • Transverse and posterior wall: obturator oblique view demonstrates transverse fracture line and posterior wall fragment
    • T-type: combines a transverse fracture with a vertical fracture that divides the ischiopubic fragment into two pieces
      • Vertical component is best visualized on the obturator oblique view
    • Anterior column/posterior hemitransverse: combines an anterior wall/column fracture with a transverse fracture of the posterior column
    • Both columns: both columns are separated from each other and the axial skeleton, resulting in a floating acetabulum
      • No portion of the articular surface remains in continuity with the axial skeleton
      • Spur sign is pathognomonic
    Associated patterns
  • OTA: acetabulum (62)
    Type A: partial articular, one column
    A1: posterior wall
    A2: posterior column
    A3: anterior
    Type B: partial articular, transverse
    B1: transverse
    B2: T-shaped
    B3: anterior column, posterior hemitransverse
    Type C: complete articular, both columns
    C1: high
    C2: low
    C3: sacroiliac joint involvement
