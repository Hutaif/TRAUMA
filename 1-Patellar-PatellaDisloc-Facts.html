Facts
  • Largest sesamoid bone in the human body
  • Dislocations are associated with direct blows, particularly during sudden changes in direction (e.g. cutting maneuver)

  • Patella increases lever arm and extensor moment of the knee extensor mechanism
  • Joint reactive force during normal activities can equal 3x body weight and during deep knee bend up to 8x
  • Medial and lateral retinacula are strong expansions of the quadriceps that insert on the tibia
  • Lateral aspect of the femoral trochlear groove is more prominent and extends further anteriorly
  • Blood supply from geniculate arteries (popliteal)
  • Dislocation is more common in females due to physiologic laxity
  • Q angle is the angle subtended by a line between the anterior inferior iliac spine to the center of the patella and a line between the center of the patella to the center of the tibial tubercle
    • Normal Q angle is 14° (male) and 17° (female) of valgus
  • Static stabilizers:
    • Medial patellofemoral ligament (MPFL): major soft tissue restraint to lateral displacement
    • Medial patellotibial ligament
    • Medial patellomeniscal ligament
  • Dynamic stabilizers:
    • Vastus medialis oblique (VMO): provides medially directed stabilizing force during extension
    • Quadriceps
  • Dislocations are associated with patella alta (high riding patella), hypoplasia of the trochlea or vastus medialis, increased Q angle, increased femoral anteversion, genu valgum
    • Lateral: most common, due to forced IR of the femur on an externally rotated and planted tibia
    • Medial: rare, usually iatrogenic or associated with congenital abnormality
    • Intraarticular: very rare, result from quadriceps avulsion
    • Superior: very rare, result from osteophyte impingement

  • Patients present with knee effusion, inability to flex the knee and obvious deformity
  • Patellar dislocations can spontaneously relocate prior to presentation leaving few signs of dislocation
    • Apprehension test: laterally directed force on the reduced patella in the extended position reproduces sensation of impending dislocation
  • Tenderness to palpation along the medial patella suggests ligamentous disruption
  • Assess NV status and identify associated injuries
  • Chondral injuries are common
