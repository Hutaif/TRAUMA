Imaging
    Radiographs
  • AP, lateral and oblique views of the foot are standard examinations
  • Weight-bearing views: provide more information about ligament status than non-weight-bearing views
  • AP:
    • Medial border of the 2nd MT should be colinear with the medial border of the middle cuneiform
    • 1st-2nd intermetatarsal space at the TMT joint should be equal to the space between the medial and middle cuneiforms
  • Oblique:
    • Medial border of the 4th MT should be colinear with the medial border of the cuboid
    • 2nd-3rd intermetatarsal space at the TMT joint should be equal to the space between the medial and middle cuneiforms
  • Lateral:
    • Dorsal displacement of the MTs implies ligamentous injury

  • Fleck fractures about the base of the 2nd MT, particularly in the 1-2 intermetatarsal space, imply a Lisfranc injury
  • Stress views: consider obtaining if patient cannot tolerate weight-bearing
    • AP with abduction/adduction and lateral with plantarflexion/dorsiflexion
    • Diastatsis of > 2 mm suggests ligamentous instability

  • CT: useful for identifying occult fractures and dislocations
