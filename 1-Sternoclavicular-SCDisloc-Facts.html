Facts
  • SC dislocations are rare
  • Commonly due to either a direct blow or indirectly through an axial load to an abducted upper extremity

  • Medial clavicle epiphysis does not close until mid-twenties
  • Anterior dislocation twice as common and posterior
  • Posterior dislocation can threaten trachea, esophagus, subclavian and great vessels
  • SC joint components:
    • Capsule: anterior is stronger than posterior
    • Costoclavicular ligament: runs from 1st rib to clavicle, restricting posterior and superior motion
    • Interclavicular ligament: runs between clavicles and anchors to the superior manubrium
    • Intraarticular disc

  • Patients present with deformity and pain
  • Pain is exacerbated by movement of the arm
  • Assess NV status and identify associated injuries
  • Must examine for dysphagia, dyspnea, hoarseness of voice, venous congestion and carotid bruits in addition to routine neurovascular examination
