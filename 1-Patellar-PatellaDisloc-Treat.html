Treatment
  • Nonoperative:
    • Patella can be reduced with manual pressure applied in against the direction of deformity with knee extended
    • Cast/brace in extension for 3 weeks with WBAT, followed by ROM and quadriceps strengthening
    • Total immobilization time should be 6-8 weeks, at the end of which the immobilization can be weaned

  • Operative: indications include recurrent dislocations or displaced intraarticular fractures
    • Patellar malalignment that leads to instability must be corrected
    • Patellar fractures can be fixed using tension band with K-wires or modified tension band with wire through cannulated cancellous lag screws (see Patella Fracture chapter)
    • Proximal instability procedures:
      • Lateral release: reduces lateralizing force
      • Medial retinaculum plication: helps centralize the patella
      • Repair of the MPFL: restores static stabilizer
      • VMO plasty: advancement distally on patella
    • Distal instability procedures: reorient the patellar ligament and tibial tubercle in patients with high Q angles
      • Advance and medialize the tubercle, correcting patella alta and normalizing the Q angle

Complications
  • Knee stiffness: most often due to prolonged immobilization
  • Patellofemoral pain: typically due to retinacular disruption at the time of dislocation leading to disrupted patellar tracking or chondral injury
  • Redislocation: lower risk when initial dislocation occurs at ages > 20 years
    • Recurrence is an indication for operative intervention
