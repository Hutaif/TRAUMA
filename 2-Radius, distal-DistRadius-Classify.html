Classification
  • Descriptive:
    Open or closed
    Displacement
    Angulation
    Comminution
    Loss of radial height

  • Frykman:
    Type I: extraarticular
    Type II: extraarticular with ulnar styloid fracture
    Type III: radiocarpal intraarticular
    Type IV: radiocarpal intraarticular with ulnar styloid fracture
    Type V: radioulnar intraarticular
    Type VI: radioulnar intraarticular with ulnar styloid fracture
    Type VII: radiocarpal and radioulnar intraarticular
    Type VIII: radiocarpal and radioulnar intraarticular with ulnar styloid fracture
    • Frykman classification
  • Melone: four part articular fracture based on lunate impaction injury
    Type I: stable, no comminution
    Type II: "die punch" fracture (lunate impaction on lunate facet) with volar or dorsal displacement
    Type III: volar metaphyseal spike (contuses volar soft tissue structures)
    Type IV: lunate facet fragments displace divergently dorsal and volar
    Type V: severe comminution
    • Melone's four articular fracture fragments
  • Intraarticular fracture: based on parts
    Two part: dorsal or volar rim (Barton's)
    Three part: lunate and scaphoid facets separate from each other and proximal radius
    Four part: three part with lunate facet fractured additionally into dorsal and volar fragments (analogous to Melone fracture pattern)
    Five part: highly comminuted

  • Fernandez: distal radioulnar joint (DRUJ) lesions associated with distal radius fractures
    Type I: stable, DRUJ reduced
    A: ulnar styloid tip fracture
    B: ulnar neck fracture
    Type II: unstable, DRUJ subluxed
    A: triangular fibrocartilage complex (TFCC) tear
    B: ulnar styloid base fracture
    Type III: potentially unstable
    A: intraarticular fracture of the sigmoid notch
    B: intraarticular fracture of the ulnar head

  • OTA: radius/ulna, distal segment (23)
    Type A: extraarticular
    A1: extraarticular ulna, radius intact
    A2: extraarticular radius, ulna intact
    A3: extraarticular, multifragmentary radius fracture
    Type B: partial articular fracture of radius
    B1: sagittal
    B2: dorsal rim (Barton's)
    B3: volar rim (reverse Barton)
    Type C: complete articular fracture of radius
    C1: simple articular and metaphyseal
    C2: simple articular and metaphyseal multifragmentary
    C3: multifragmentary articular

  • Eponyms:
    Colles fracture:
    • Traditionally defined as extraarticular fracture of distal radius with apex volar angulation
    • 90% of distal radius fractures

    Smith's fracture:
    • Extraarticular fracture with apex dorsal angulation
    • Nortoriously unstable fracture pattern

    Barton's fracture:
    • Fracture-dislocation/subluxation of the carpus in which dorsal or volar rim of the distal radius is displaced along with carpus and hand
    • Volar pattern is more common
    • Commonly unstable

    Chauffeur's fracture:
    • Avulsion fracture of the radial styloid
    • Caused by compression of scaphoid against styloid
    • Often associated with intercarpal ligamentous injuries
