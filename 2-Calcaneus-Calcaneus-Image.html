Imaging
    Radiographs
  • Standard films include AP and lateral of the foot, Harris view and ankle series
  • AP view of the foot: will demonstrate calcaneocuboid extension and lateral wall blowout
  • Lateral view of the foot:
    • Böhler's angle: measured as the angle subtended by two lines - one between the superior-most aspect of the anterior process to the superior-most aspect of the posterior articular surface and from the superior-most aspect of the posterior articular surface to the posterior tuberosity
      • The supplement of this angle is normally between 25°-40°
      • Lower supplements indicate collapse of the posterior facet and correlate with worse outcome
    • Gissane's (crucial) angle: formed by the angle subtended by a line along the posterior facet and another along the beak of the calcaneus
      • Normally 100°
      • Increase in angle indicates collapse of the posterior facet


    Angles of Böhler and Gissane

  • Harris axial view: foot in maximum dorsiflexion, beam angled at 45°
    • Visualizes the subtalar joint, loss of calcaneal height, widening and impingement on the peroneal space laterally
    • Normal AP foot: yellow dotted line highlights normal TMT joint alignment

  • Broden views: leg internally rotated 30°, ankle in neutral
    • Views are taken at 10°, 20°, 30° and 40° cephalad
    • Visualizes the posterior facet, useful for evaluating reduction intraoperatively
  • Lumbar spine films should also be considered based on mechanism of injury

  • CT: standard examination for articular fractures
    • Coronals allow visualization of the posterior facet articular surface, sustentaculum tali, widening, varus alignment and FHL tendons
    • Axials demonstrate the calcaneocuboid joint, the anteroinferior aspect of the posterior facet and the sustentaculum tali
