Treatment
  • Nonoperative: indicated for intact extensor mechnanism (most important), displacement < 3 mm with articular step-off < 2 mm
    • Cylinder cast or hinged knee brace locked in extension for 4-6 weeks and early weight-bearing

  • Operative: indications include open fractures, displacement > 3 mm, articular step-off > 2 mm, disrupted extensor mechanism and incarcerated fragments within the joint
    • As with all open fractures, thorough debridement is required when open wounds are present
    • Tension band with K-wires or modified tension band with wire through cannulated cancellous lag screws
    • Circumferential cerclage wires may be useful for stellate fractures and osteoporotic bone
    • Retinacular disruption should be repaired
    • Test flexion in the OR to determine safe ROM without fracture gapping
    • Immobilization for 1-6 weeks depending on security of fixation and degree of comminution followed by ROM exercises
    • Partial or total patellectomy may be performed when comminution prevents repair
      • Inferior pole most commonly excised
      • Advance patellar tendon to remaining patella
      • Total patellectomy reduces quadriceps torque by 50%

Complications
  • Knee stiffness: due to prolonged immobilization
    • May benefit from manipulation under anesthesia
  • Nonunion (2%): absence of bridging bone at 4 months
    • Consider revision osteosynthesis (young, active patient) or partial patellectomy
  • Osteonecrosis: associated with higher degrees of displacement and typically afflicts proximal fragment
    • Spontaneous revascularization can usually be expected by 2 years
  • Posttraumatic arthritis (50%): most can be managed conservatively
    • Distal patellar realignment procedures and patellectomy are options for intractable pain
  • Refracture (1-5%): due to residual weakness in bone at fracture site
  • Symptomatic hardware: commonly associated with tension band construct
    • Removal of hardward generally resolves symptoms
