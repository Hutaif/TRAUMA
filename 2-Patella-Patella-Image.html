Imaging
    Radiographs
  • AP/lateral: identify fracture pattern and displacement
    • Insall-Salvati ratio: measured on lateral, it is the ratio of the length of the patella to the length of the patellar tendon (measured from the distal pole of the patella to the tibial tubercle)
    • Normal Insall-Salvati ratio is 1.0
    • Patella alta (Insall-Salvati ratio < 0.8): high riding patella in the setting of trauma suggests patellar tendon disruption
    • Patella baja: low riding patella in the setting of trauma suggests quadriceps tendon disruption
  • AP: patella should be centralized in trochlea of the femur
  • Lateral: with the knee flexed to 90°, the superior pole of the patella should lie below the anterior surface of the femoral diaphysis
    • Normal AP knee
    Normal lateral knee

  • Sunrise: assists in evaluation of articular surface and vertical fractures (but difficult to obtain)

  • CT: evaluates articular and vertical fractures without having to flex the knee
  • MRI: useful for identifying osteochondral fractures
