Classification
  • Essex-Lopresti: articular fractures - mechanism of injury consistently produces two fracture lines
    • Primary fracture line: posteromedial to anterolaterally
      • Produces two main fragments: sustentacular (anteromedial) and tuberosity (posterolateral)
      • Anteromedial fragment rarely comminutes attached to the talus by deltoid and interosseous talocalcaneal ligaments
      • Posterolateral fragment usually displaces superolaterally causing shortening, widening and posterior facet incongruity
    • Secondary fracture line: begins at Gissane's angle, extends under posterior facet and extends posteriorly
      • Tongue fracture: exits posteriorly
      • Joint depression fracture: exits behind the posterior facet and dissociates the posterior facet from the tuberosity
    • Further compressive force causes the anteromedial fragment to migrate further medially, increasing the widening and shortening of the calcaneus
      • Tuberosity fragment then rotates into varus
      • Lateral wall can blow out causing impingement of the peroneal tendons
    • Essex-Lopresti secondary fracture lines
  • Sanders: based on number and location of articular fractures as determined from the coronal CT slice that captures the posterior facet at its widest dimension
    • Three fracture lines: A (lateral), B (middle) and C (medial)
    • Four fragments: lateral, central, medial, sustentacular
    Type I: all nondisplaced
    Type II: two-part fracture of the posterior facet
    IIA: lateral fracture line
    IIB: central fracture line
    IIC: medial fracture line
    Type III: three-part fracture of the posterior facet with centrally depressed fragment
    IIIA: lateral and central fracture lines
    IIIB: lateral and medial fracture lines
    IIIC: central and medial fracture lines
    Type IV: four-part articular fracture, highly comminuted
    • Sanders classification
  • OTA: calcaneus (82)
    Type A: avulsion, process or tuberosity
    A1: anterior process
    A2: sustentaculum
    A3: tuberosity
    Type B: nonarticular body
    B1: noncomminuted
    B2: comminuted
    Type C: articular, involving posterior facet
    C1: two-part
    C2: three-part
    C3: four+ parts

  • Other fractures:
    Anterior process:
    • Due to plantarflexion and inversion
      • Tightens bifurcate and interosseous ligaments leading to avulsion fracture

    Medial process: vertical sheer due to axial loading with hindfoot in valgus

    Sustentaculum tali: hindfoot loading with foot inversion
    • Can be misdiagnosed as an ankle sprain

    Tuberosity: due to avulsion of the Achilles

    Body fractures not involving the subtalar articulation: due to axial loading
    • Can cause significant widening, comminution and loss of height
