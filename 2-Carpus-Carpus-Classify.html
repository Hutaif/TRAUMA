  • Location:
    Proximal third: 20%
    Middle third (waist): 75%
    Distal third: 5%
    • Scaphoid fractures by location
  • Stability:
    Stable: no step off in any plane
    Unstable: displacement > 1 mm, scapholunate angle > 60° or radiolunate angle > 15°

  • Herbert:
    Type A: stable acute
    A1: tubercle (distal pole)
    A2: incomplete waist
    Type B: unstable acute
    B1: distal third oblique
    B2: displaced waist
    B3: proximal pole
    B4: transcaphoid carpal fracture-dislocation
    B5: comminuted
    Type C: delayed union
    Type D: nonunion
    D1: fibrous
    D2: displaced

    Lunate:
  • Stahl and Lichtman: progression of Kienböck's disease
    Stage I: normal appearance or linear or compression fracture on CT
    Stage II: sclerosis, slight collapse of radial border
    Stage III: fragmentation, collapse, cystic degeneration, loss of carpal height, proximal migration of capitate, scaphoid rotation
    Stage IV: advanced collapse, scaphoid rotation, sclerosis, radiocarpal ostephytes
