Treatment
  • Urgent reduction is warranted to avoid skin necrosis and neurovascular injury

  • Closed Reduction:
    • Conscious sedation or general anesthesia should be utilized
    • Relax achilles tendon with knee flexion
    • Inversion may be required for medial dislocations and eversion for lateral dislocations to disengage the calcaneus
    • Traction with direct pressure over the talar head can usually lead to reduction of medial dislocations
    • Immobilize for 3 weeks followed by gentle ROM exercises

  • Operative: indications include open dislocation, operative fractures and failed closed reduction
    • Can be performed through an anteromedial incision
    • Blocks to reduction can be addressed through this exposure
    • For treatment of associated fractures, see respective chapter

  • Assess reduction radiographically and evaluate stability
    • Temporary smooth K-wires can be placed across the joints for stabilization if necessary
  • Immobilize for 3-6 weeks in a SLC and protected weight-bearing

Complications
  • Osteonecrosis of the talus
  • Posttraumatic arthritis: a consequence of any articular injury, can be theoretically minimized with anatomic reduction
    • Severe symptoms can be treated with arthrodesis
  • Recurrent instability: uncommon
  • Subtalar stiffness
