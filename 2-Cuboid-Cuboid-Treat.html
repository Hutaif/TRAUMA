Treatment
  • Objective: restore articular congruity (calcaneocuboid and cubometatarsal) and maintain lateral column length

  • Nonoperative: indicated for nondisplaced, stable fractures (as demonstrated by weight-bearing films) and maintenance of the lateral column length
    • NWB SLC for 6 weeks followed by slow progressive weight-bearing
    • Avulsion fractures can be treated with a walking boot

  • Operative: indications include displacement > 2 mm, articular involvement or instability (as demonstrated by weight-bearing films)
    • Mini-fragment screws and plating can be used to support lateral column
    • Bone graft severe impaction injuries
    • Severe comminution may require arthrodesis to recreate foot alignment
    • Instability involving the 4th and 5th MTs are considered Lisfranc injuries and require fixation (see Tarsometatarsal Dislocation chapter)

Complications
  • Loss of lateral column length: may require bone grafting, fusion of the calcaneocuboid joint or open wedge osteotomy
  • Nonunion: may require revision ORIF with bone grafting
  • Osteonecrosis: may result from severe comminution or displacement
  • Postraumatic arthritis: due to persistent articular noncongruity, initial chondral damage or articular comminution
