Classification
  • Anatomic:
    Lateral process
    Posterior process (Shepard's fracture)
    Talar head, neck, body

  • Hawkins: talar neck (represents 50% of all talar fractures)
    Type I: nondisplaced (0-10% chance of osteonecrosis)
    Type II: associated subtalar subluxation or dislocation (20-50% chance of osteonecrosis)
    Type III: associated subtalar and ankle dislocation (80-100% chance of osteonecrosis)
    Type IV: type III with talonavicular subluxation/dislocation
    • Hawkins classification
  • OTA: talus (81)
    Type A: avulsion, process or head
    A1: avulsion
    A2: process
    A3: head
    Type B: neck
    B1: nondisplaced
    B2: displaced with subtalar subluxation
    B3: displaced with subtalar and ankle subluxation
    Type C: body
    C1: tibiotalar involvement
    C2: subtalar involvement
    C3: tibiotalar and subtalarinvolvement
