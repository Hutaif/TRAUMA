Classification
  • Descriptive:
    Location of the femoral head, presence of fractures

  • Thompson and Epstein: posterior dislocations
    Type I: simple
    Type II: dislocation with single large posterior wall fragment
    Type III: dislocation with a comminuted posterior wall fragment
    Type IV: dislocation with fracture of the acetabular floor
    Type V: dislocation with fracture of the femoral head

  • Epstein: anterior dislocations
    Type I: superior
    IA: no associated fractures
    IB: associated fracture or impaction of the femoral head
    IC: associated fracture of the acetabulum
    Type II: inferior
    IIA: no associated fractures
    IIB: associated fracture or impaction of the femoral head
    IIC: associated fracture of the acetabulum

  • Comprehensive:
    Type I: no associated fractures, no clinical instability post-reduction
    Type II: irreducible dislocation, no significant femoral head or acetabular fracture (general anesthesia needed)
    Type III: unstable hip post-reduction or with incarcerated elements
    Type IV: associated acetabular fracture requiring reconstruction
    Type V: associated femoral neck or head injury

  • OTA: hip (30)
    1. Anterior
    2. Posterior
    3. Medial (through acetabulum)
    4. Obturator
    5. Other
