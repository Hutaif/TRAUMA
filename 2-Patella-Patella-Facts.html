Facts
  • Largest sesamoid bone in the human body
  • Two mechanisms of injury:
    • Rapid flexion against contracted quadriceps typically results in simple transverse fractures
    • Direct blow results in a variety of fracture patterns including stellate and vertical

  • Quadriceps insert on the superior pole
  • Patellar tendon extends from inferior pole to tibial tubercle
  • Superior 2/3 of the posterior surface is covered in articular cartilage
  • 3 facets: medial, lateral and odd
    • Odd facet is a small strip medial to the medial facet that engages femur only at full flexion
  • Medial and lateral retinacula are strong expansions of the quadriceps tendon that insert on the tibia
    • Active extension can be preserved through these structures despite patella fracture
    • Displacement of patellar fracture fragments implies degree of injury to these structures
  • Blood supply from geniculate arteries (popliteal)
  • Bipartite patella (1%) may be mistaken for a fracture
    • Usually exists in the superolateral position and has smooth margins
    • Bilateral in 50%
  • Patella increases lever arm and extensor moment of the knee extensor mechanism
  • Joint reactive force during normal activities can equal 3x body weight and during deep knee bend up to 8x

  • Patients present with pain, swelling and variable degrees of weakness
  • A palpable defect may be identified with displaced fractures
  • Nondisplaced fractures may demonstrate only point tenderness
  • Test extensor mechanism with active straight leg raise
    • Inability to straight leg raise suggests disruption of both the medial and lateral retinacula
    • Intact extensor mechanism allows consideration of nonoperative treatment
  • Assess NV status and identify associated injuries
  • Disruptions to the quadriceps and patellar tendons may be present
  • Open fractures must be ruled out in the presence of any skin lacerations
    • A saline injection into the joint can be used to determine continuity with lacerations
    • Perform arthrocentesis (remove hemarthrosis) followed by injection of saline (~50 cc)
    • Extravasation from the wound indicates continuity with the joint and open fracture
    • Presence of an open fracture requires urgent surgical debridement
  • Must also suspect fractures of the ipsilateral femur, proximal tibia and dislocation of the hip with high-energy injuries
