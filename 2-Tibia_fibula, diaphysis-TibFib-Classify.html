Classification
  • Descriptive:
    Open or closed
    Anatomic location (proximal, middle, distal third)
    Fracture pattern: comminution, butterfly, spiral, transverse, oblique
    Angulation
    Shortening
    Displacement: % cortical contact
    Rotation
    Associated injuries

  • Gustillo-Anderson: describes soft tissue injury in open tibial shaft fractures
    Grade I: open wound < 1 cm in size with minimal surrounding soft tissue injury
    • Fracture is usually simple
    • Wound bed is clean
    Grade II: open wound > 1 cm in size with moderate surrounding soft tissue injury
    • Fracture usually demonstrates moderate comminuition
    • Wound bed is moderately contaminated
    Grade III: extensive soft tissue damage and/or contamination
    • Includes all open segmental, gunshot, farm injuries with soil contamination and traumatic amputations
    • IIIA: adequate local soft tissue coverage
    • IIIB: extensive soft tissue loss requiring regional or free flap
    • IIIC: major vascular injury requiring repair for limb salvage

  • Tscherne and Gotzen: describes soft tissue injury in closed tibial shaft fractures
    Grade 0: minimal injury
    • Associated with simple fracture pattern
    Grade 1: abrasion or contusion with moderate swelling
    • Associated with moderate energy fracture pattern
    Grade 2: deep contaminated abrasion with substantial soft tissue welling
    • Associated with high-energy fracture pattern and pending compartment syndrome
    Grade 3: extensive contusion or crush with compartment syndrome

  • OTA: tibia/fibula, diaphyseal (42)
    Type A: simple
    A1: spiral
    A2: oblique (> 30°)
    A3: transverse (< 30°)
    Type B: wedge/butterfly
    B1: spiral
    B2: bending
    B3: fragmented
    Type C: complex/comminuted
    C1: spiral
    C2: segmented
    C3: irregular
