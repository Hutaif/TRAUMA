Treatment
  • Closed reduction should be attempted:
    • Flex knee to 90° and apply pressure to fibular head to correct the deformity
    • Superior dislocations require concomitant ankle inversion
    • Reductions are usually stable

  • Nonoperative: generally preferred
    • With stable closed reduction, short course of immobilization with a knee brace and protected weight-bearing for < 2 weeks is appropriate
    • Early ROM and progression to weight-bearing follows resolution of symptoms

  • Operative: indications include irreducible dislocations, fractures about the PTFJ and open wounds
    • Options include pin fixation, arthrodesis and fibular head resection

Complications
  • Peroneal nerve palsy (5%): usually transient and resolve upon joint reduction
    • Nerve palsies that develop or worsen after manipulation require exploration
  • Posttraumatic arthritis: a consequence of any articular injury, can be theoretically minimized with anatomic reduction
    • Generally rare in PTFJ dislocations
