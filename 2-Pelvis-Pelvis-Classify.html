Classification
  • Young and Burgess: pelvis based on mechanism
    Anteroposterior compression (APC): anterior force resulting in hemipelvis ER, symphysis diastasis and rami fractures
    • APC I: < 2.5 cm of symphyseal diastasis, vertical rami fractures, posterior ligaments intact
    • APC II: > 2.5 cm of symphyseal diastasis, widening of the SI joints with disruption of ligaments but intact posterior sacroiliac ligaments (open book injury)
      • External and internal rotational instability
      • Vertical stability maintained
    • APC III: symphyseal, sacroiliac, sacrospinous and sacrotuberous ligaments completely disrupted
      • Completely unstable with high rate of neurovascular compromise and blood loss
    Lateral compression (LC): compression of pelvis due to laterally applied force that shortens the sacroiliac, sacrospinous and sacrotuberous ligaments and result in fractures to the anterior and posterior pelvis
    • LC I: sacral impaction ipsilateral to impact often with transverse pubic rami fractures
    • LC II: posterior iliac wing fractures ipsilateral to impact with variable disruption to posterior ligaments
      • Stable to vertical and ER stress
    • LC III: LC I or LC II injury with ER of contralateral hemipelvis (windswept pelvis) due to sacroiliac, sacrospinous and sacrotuberous ligament disruption
    Vertical shear: vertical force applied to extended lower extremity or innominate bone
    • Complete disruption of symphyseal, sacroiliac, sacrospinous and sacrotuberous ligaments
    • Hemipelvis is vertically displaced
    • High risk of hypovolemic shock (63%) and death (25%)
    Combined: typically vertical shear and lateral compression due to crush injuries
    • Young and Burgess classification
  • Tile: pelvis based on stability
    Type A: stable
    A1: fractures of the pelvis not involving the ring (avulsion)
    A2: stable, minimal displacement
    Type B: rotationally unstable, vertically stable
    B1: ER instability, open book
    B2: LC, internal rotational instability, ipsilateral only
    B3: LC, internal rotational instability, bilateral
    Type C: rotationally and vertically unstable
    C1: unilateral
    C2: bilateral, one rotationally unstable, the other vertically unstable
    C3: bilateral, both rotationally and vertically unstable, with acetabular fracture

  • OTA: pelvic ring (61)
    Type A: stable fracture of pelvic ring
    A1: avulsion fracture of innominate bone
    A2: direct impact fracture of innominate bone
    A3: transverse fracture of sacrum and coccyx
    Type B: partially stable fracture of pelvic ring
    B1: unilateral, partial disruption of the posterior ligaments, ER (open book)
    B2: unilateral, partial disruption of the posterior ligaments, IR (compression)
    B3: bilateral, partial disruption of the posterior ligaments
    Type C: unstable fracture of the pelvic ring
    C1: unilateral, complete disruption of posterior ligaments
    C2: bilateral, one side with complete disruption of posterior ligaments
    C3: bilateral, both sides with complete disruption of posterior ligaments

  • Denis: sacral fractures based on anatomy
    Zone 1: sacral ala - lateral to neuroforamina
    • Can cause L4 and L5 nerve root impingement as they cross the sacral ala
    • 6% have neurologic deficits
    Zone 2: through neuroforamina
    • Can cause L5, S1 or S2 nerve root impingement
    • 28% have neurologic deficits
    Zone 3: body or central
    • Highest risk of cauda equina and neurogenic bladder
    • 56% have neurologic deficits
    Transverse: higher incidence of bladder dysfunction when S1, S2 and/or S3 segment is involved
