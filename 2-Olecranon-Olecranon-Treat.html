Treatment
  • Nonoperative: indicated for nondisplaced fractures (< 2 mm)
    • Long arm posterior splint in acute phase with elbow in 60°-90° of flexion
      • Gradual introduction of ROM at 1-2 weeks.
    • Consider LAC in unreliable patients
      • Cast can be removed prior to 3 weeks to begin ROM

  • Operative: indicated for displaced fractures > 2 mm
    • Direct posterior approach to olecranon can be utilized
    • Tension band with two K-wires can be used for transverse or oblique fracture patterns proximal to coronoid
      • Comminution or patterns that risk compression/distraction of the oleranon fossa are contraindications to this technique
    • Intramedullary lag screw fixation: can be used in place of wires in tension band construct
    • Bicortical screw fixation: oblique or transverse fractures at the coronoid
    • Plate fixation: displaced comminution, longitudinal oblique or fractures that extend past coronoid
    • Excision of up to 50% of the olecranon with triceps advancement can be considered for highly comminuted fractures
    • Fractures of the radial head and coronoid associated with fracture-dislocations can typically be treated through the olecranon exposure (see Elbow Dislocation chapter)

Complications
  • Decreased extension power: due to lengthening of olecranon and shortening of triceps
  • Elbow stiffness: loss of extension (10°-15°) can complicate up to 50% of cases
  • Nonunion (1-5%): treatment options include revision osteosynthesis, excision, bone graft or arthroplasty
  • Painful hardware: up to 80% of cases, more common with tension band wiring
  • Posttraumatic arthritis: a consequence of any articular injury, can be theoretically minimized with anatomic reduction
  • Ulnar nerve symptoms (up to 10%): can occur due to callus compression
