Classification
  • Descriptive:
    Dislocation or subluxation
    Direction: anterior, posterior, inferior, superior
    Chronology: acute, chronic, recurrent
    Force: atraumatic, traumatic
    Patient contribution: voluntary, involuntary
    Associated fractures

  • Anterior subtypes:
    Subcorocoid (majority): humeral head is translated anteriorly, inferior to the corocoid
    • Greater tuberosity (commonly fractured) can be wedged on glenoid rim
    • Scapula is often abducted
    Subglenoid (33%): humeral head is anterior and inferior to glenoid
    • Associated with anterior-inferior glenoid fractures and greater tuberosity fractures
    Subclavicular (rare): humeral head is medial to the coracoid, inferior to the clavicle

  • Posterior subtypes:
    Subacromial (common): articular surface directed posteriorly, lesser tuberosity occupies glenoid fossa
    Subglenoid (rare): humeral head posterior and inferior to glenoid
    Subspinus (rare): humeral head medial to acromion and inferior to scapular spine

  • OTA: glenohumeral (10-A)
    1. Anterior
    2. Posterior
    3. Lateral (theoretical)
    4. Medial (theoretical)
    5. Other (inferior)
