Imaging
    Radiographs
  • AP, lateral views of the forearm, wrist and elbow should be obtained
  • AP: radial tuberosity and the radial styloid should point in opposite directions on the AP radiograph, otherwise there is a rotational component to the fracture
  • Lateral: radius and ulna should be straight
    • Identify DRUJ or radial head subluxation or dislocation
    • Normal AP (left) and lateral (right) forearm
  • CT: useful for radioulnar joint assessment
