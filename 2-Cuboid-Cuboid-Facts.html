Facts
  • Isolated injuries are rare
  • Can occur after simple twisting of the ankle or after high-energy injury
  • Commonly injured as part of Lisfranc or talonavicular joint dislocation

  • Articulates with calcaneus proximally, navicular and lateral cuneiform medially and 4th/5th MTs distally
    • Longitudinal plantar ligament supports calcaneocuboid joint
  • Peroneus longus runs plantar to the cuboid (where fracture callus can irritate)
  • Two column concept: structural description of the foot
    • Medial: 1st, 2nd and 3rd MTs, the 3 cuneiforms, navicular and talus
    • Lateral: 4th and 5th MTs, cuboid and calcaneus
  • Nutcracker injury: torsional stress or forefoot abduction causes compression between MTs and anterior process of the calcaneus
  • Stress fractures can occur, particularly in athletes
  • Untreated cuboid fracture: lateral column of the foot can shorten and destabilize, resulting in pes planus (flatfoot deformity) and altered weight-bearing of the foot

  • Patients present with pain in the lateral foot with variable ecchymosis and swelling
  • Assess NV status and identify associated injuries
  • Often associated with talonavicular joint and Lisfranc injuries
