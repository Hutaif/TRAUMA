Treatment
  • Generally require operative treatment to achieve anatomic alignment and stable fixation

  • Nonoperative: indicated for minimally displaced (< 2 mm), without fracture and stable to radiographic stress
    • NWB SLC for 6 weeks

  • Operative: generally preferred for any medically stable patient with instability (> 2 mm of displacement)
    • Massive swelling should be treated initially with provisional closed reduction to protect the soft tissue from further damage
      • Reduction can be achieved with manual manipulation to reverse deformity under sufficient anesthesia
      • Occasionally reduction can be prevented by interposition of soft tissue
      • Reduction is followed by splinting and elevation with monitoring for compartment syndrome
    • ORIF beginning medially, focused on reducing the 2nd MT base
      • Two longitudinal incisions: one over 1st intermetatarsal space and the other base of the 4th MT
      • Fixation achieved with K-wires or screws
      • Bone grafting can be used for impaction fractures
    • Stiffness is tolerated given limited physiologic motion
    • SLC NWB for 6-8 weeks followed by a walking cast for 4-6 weeks

Complications
  • Compartment syndrome: requires urgent fasciotomies to release pressure and avoid muscle death
  • Posttraumatic arthritis: a consequence of any articular injury, can be theoretically minimized with anatomic reduction
    • Can be amenable to arch supports but may require arthrodesis
