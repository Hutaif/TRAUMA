Classification
  • Anatomic:
    Cortical avulsion (47%): due to excessive plantarflexion or eversion of the midfoot
    • Dorsal lip avulsion by the talonavicular capsule, deltoid ligament or naviculocuneiform ligaments
    Tuberosity fractures (20%): due to acute eversion causing avulsion of the tuberosity by the posterior tibial tendon insertion or deltoid
    • Associated with the "nutcracker injury" to the cuboid and corresponding anterior process calcaneus fracture
    Body fractures (29%)
    Stress fractures: require MRI or bone scan for diagnosis

  • Sangeorzan: includes avulsion, tuberosity and 3 types of body fractures
    Type I: transverse fracture line in coronal plane, minimal comminution, dorsal displacement of the free fragment, medial foot normal on AP radiograph
    Type II: most common, fracture line from dorsal-lateral to plantar-medial with talonavicular joint disruption, dorsomedial displacement of the fracture fragment
    Type III: comminuted fracture with naviculocuneiform joint disruption

  • OTA: navicular (83)
    Type A: simple
    Type B: comminuted
